Never finish all of your food

Description

In Taiwan there is a tradition where Taiwanese people serve a lot of food to their guest but doesn't expect them to finish all of it. If the guest does somehow finish and leave an empty bowl, it means that they didn't serve enough food and the guests are still hungry. Although in may other counties, an empty bowl may mean that the guests appreciate your cooking and that your cooking was good but in Taiwan it means that the people didn't serve their guests well enough.

The Dragon Boat Festival is said to be closetly related to the remembrance of Qu Yuan, a person living in the Warring States period. The most common customs during this festival is racing dragon boats. Dragon boats are around 40 feet long and can hold up to 22 people. There are a total of 20 paddlers plus another person to steer the boat and a drummer located at the front of the boat near the dragons head. All of the boats weigh around 1500 pounds so it takes a lot of effort to move it across the water.

Lantern Festival

Description

The Lantern Festival is a celebration of the coming of spring. It's also the first day of the new year where there's a full moon. The history of this festival dates back to the Qing dynasty. A group of villagers would go to the mountains during the winter to avoid having to deal with bandits, when the worst of the winter has passed, sky lanterns were flown up as a signal saying that it was safe in the village now. The lanterns were also thought to be able to remove bad luck and make your wishes come true.
